Parallel Programming on the GPU

Assignment 2: Synchronization

Objective

This assignment will help you understand synchronization primitives used in CUDA programming

Problem Statement

Given a 2D matrix of floaing point numbers perform the following operations in sequence.
  1. Swap elements at even and odd positions of the lower triangular matrix (including the principal diagonal)
  2. Reflect the values of the matrix across the principal diagonal i.e. A[j][i]=A[i][j] where i is not equal to j.
$$ \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 6 &9 \\ 2 & 8 &3 \\ 4 &12 &2 \end{pmatrix} --> \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 6 &9 \\ 8 & 2 &3 \\ 12 &4 &2 \end{pmatrix} --> \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 8 &12 \\ 8 & 8 &4 \\ 12 &4 &2 \end{pmatrix}$$ Your code should have provisions to randomly generate a matrix of any size and perform the above two operations.
